  • Welcome to the class!
  • Get to know each other by meeting a partner. Collect the following information about your partner, then present it to the class.
    1. Name
    2. School
    3. Grade level
    4. Favorite thing about computers
    5. Least favorite thing about computers
    6. Goal for this class
    7. Favorite animal and why
  • Watch a video: Equity in CS education
  • Check this out: Expanding Computer Science Education to All Students in SFUSD
  • Read about the first computer programmer, Ada Lovelace.
  • Read about Alan Turing, the father of computer science.
  • Break into pairs and discuss equity in computer science. Here are some questions to talk about:
    1. Do you feel that you've been given an equal exposure to technology in school as other people you know? In other words, which side of the "tech divide" are you on and why?
    2. Do you believe you could get a job in the tech industry when you graduate high school if you wanted to? Why or why not?
    3. Would you consider attending college for computer science or attending a coding bootcamp after high school? Why or why not?
    4. Do you have any concerns or fears about learning technology skills you would like to share?
    5. Growth mindset may be described as the belief that your abilities aren't fixed, and that through hard work and perseverence, we can shape our abilities. Please contrast this mindset with a fixed mindset. On the spectrum between "fixed" and "growth" mindset, where do you see yourself?
  • Coding: what is it? HTML: what is it? Let's discuss.
