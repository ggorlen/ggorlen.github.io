Class 5: Introduction to JavaScript

Today, we'll wrap up HTML/CSS and ensure everyone is satisfied with how their page looks. Feel free to continue working on your website throughout the semester. We'll dive into Javascript, which is the main focus of this course, and is the language we'll use to write our games.

The goal for the week is to learn Javascript by building a couple small apps: a program to greet a user, which we'll expand into a number guessing game and a couple of simple animations designed to make our websites interactive. We'll use the jQuery library for a sample of the power of Javascript. During the course of the next few weeks, we'll learn more JS concepts and solve various puzzles and problems in groups.


Today's topics:

  1. Spend 20 minutes working on our websites--do you know how to embed YouTube videos into your page?
  2. Recap HTML/CSS and make sure everyone is happy with their website. Discuss Bootstrap.
    • Check this out: an all-HTML Choose your own adventure game!
  3. Brief history of JavaScript, why it's worth learning, and what we'll be using it for.
  4. Introduction to programming basics with JS
    • Adding JS scripts to HTML documents and using repl.it as an interpreter for running bits of code.
    • Literals, variables, strings, numbers, and the console.log(), alert(), prompt() functions.
      • 10 minutes: Break into pairs and solve variable puzzles.
      • Write a program that greets a user!
  5. Before leaving, please shut down your computer and plug it into a power source on the cart.
