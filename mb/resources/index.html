Resources

This page contains resources for students. The primary resource is the class community--make friends in class, trade numbers, and ask your friends for help. Feel welcome to ask the instructor for help in person before, after, or during class or via email.

Becoming a super programmer isn't about knowing all the answers; it's knowing how to find answers.

- Al Sweigart

This class encourages personal research of problems, which is an essential skill in today's world, and especially applicable to programming. Almost every problem you'll encounter in programming has been solved by someone else, and a strategic search on Google should yield an answer. For auditory learners, YouTube has an enormous number of tutorials on every subject.

What should I do after this class is over?
  • Study and build projects independently using many of the resources on this page and following free online tutorials. Put your projects on Github.
  • Do an internship. Here are some resources:
    • SFUSD career resources
    • SF youth jobs resources
    • SF Public Affairs Web development internship
    • AdRoll internship
    • Google careers
    • Bloomberg Businessweek article
  • Enroll as a CS major or minor in a four year college such as UC Berkeley.
  • Enroll in two year college courses, such as those at City College of San Francisco.
  • Enroll in a coding bootcamp. Here's a breakdown of those offered in SF. There are also many available over the internet.
  • 42 has a tuition-free summer bootcamp for high school students here in the Bay.
  • Attend courses for free and hang out at Noisebridge.
  • Surf meetup.com and attend classes, talks, and hackathons in your area.
  • Get involved with Black Girls Code or Girls Who Code.
  • Teach a Mission Bit class!
