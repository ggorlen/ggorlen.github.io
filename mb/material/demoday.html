Congratulations on making it to the end of the class! This page has information for what we'll be doing as we wind down the course and suggestions for where to go next with the skills you learned.


Here's what's left to do this semester:

  • Finish projects! All of your games are looking very cool, so we'll have a blast making them even cooler in the time we have left. Try to keep all of your group members engaged!
  • Demo Day is an important event each semester where all Mission Bit students gather to share their projects. It takes place from 5-7 PM on Wednesday, May 3rd, 2017 at Unity Technologies, 30 Third Street in San Francisco. Please invite family and friends to witness your amazing accomplishments! Two groups from this class will present their work, and we'll all arrive at 4:30 PM to set up before the event starts. Please let me know if you need to leave the event early or arrive late--we'll be organizing presentation schedules soon.
  • For groups who will present on Demo Day: you have 3 minutes to speak and share your project, and we'll do a dry run of our presentations before then. It's OK if some ideas you had for your game aren't implemented in time for presentation--the important thing is that the program doesn't crash and handles whatever features do exist in the game reasonably smoothly and you have a few ideas to talk about during the presentation.
  • Presentation slideshow: we'll need to prepare a brief slideshow to show off as part of your presentation on Demo Day. Here's a template you can use.

After this class ends:

  • Please fill out a student feedback questionnaire.
  • Mission Bit is offering three summer classes: Career Prep, Intro to Web Programming (this class), and Android Game Design. I encourage everyone to participate! Sign up at Mission Bit's website. You'll have the chance to continue to develop your computer science skills in a collaborative setting. This class covered a *lot* of material and a second run can help the concepts to stick. Or forge ahead with Android or Career Prep!
  • Please stay in touch! I'm happy to offer advice on what to do next or collaborate on a little project. I'd also love to hear feedback about what I can improve on in the class, or something you really liked that I should keep doing. My email is greg [at] missionbit.com.
